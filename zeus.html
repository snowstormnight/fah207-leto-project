Exhibit 02

Zeus of Artemision

Artwork 1: Zeus of Artemision

Date: c. 460 BCE (Early Classical Period) Medium: Bronze Dimensions: 2.09 m height

Location: National Archaeological Museum, Athens

Discovery: Found in an ancient shipwreck off Cape Artemision Brand Strategy Analysis

The Zeus of Artemision is not a sculpture it is a picture that shows power in a simple way. This work of art shows a change in Greek art: it changed from being stiff and boxy to being more real and full of life. The way the god is standing with his weight moving from his back leg to his leg is a new way of posing that would later become the standard for showing power in a natural way in Western art. The Zeus of Artemision is an example of this.

Our brand analysis finds three visual things, in The Zeus of Artemision:

1. The Dynamic Pose: Zeus stands with his arms stretched out ready to throw a lightning bolt. He looks balanced and in control unlike statues of gods that show them straining. This shows that real power does not need to show effort. A simple move can control everything.

2. The Idealized PhysiqueHis face is intense. He looks like a person but also like a god. This makes him someone we can relate to but also look up to. He is like us. Stronger.

3. The Moment of Action: The second before he throws the lightning bolt is full of excitement. He is, in control of his power not controlled by it. This shows that he is a god who knows what he is doing.

Exhibit 03

Head of Zeus from Mylasa

Artwork 2: Head of Zeus from Mylasa

Date: c. 350­340 BCE (Late Classical Period)

Medium: Marble from Mt. Pentelikon near Athens

Dimensions: 48 x 26 cm (over-life-size)

Location: Museum of Fine Arts, Boston (Accession Number 04.12)

Provenance: Mylasa in Caria (southwest Turkey) Brand Strategy Analysis

This huge marble head is like a look at the famous statue of Zeus made by Phidias a long time ago. People thought that statue was one of the amazing things in the world. Even though we can't see the original anymore this head helps us understand what it looked like and shows us another side of the Zeus brand.

Our analysis of the brand finds three things that make it strong:

The Balanced Look: The hair, beard and face are perfectly symmetrical. This makes the statue look calm and powerful. It's not the face of someone who rules with fear. Someone who is wise and strong. The message is that stability and reliability are important for leadership.

The Face: The face is turned a little to the right and looks calm not angry. This is a god who doesn't need to be angry to get what he wants. He is so powerful that he doesn't need to show it. This is the face of someone who brings justice, not revenge.

The Big Size: This head is 48 cm big which is bigger than life. The size makes you pay attention and show respect. It says that this is someone important not just a regular king but someone who makes sure everything is, in order.

Modern connection

Campaign Relevance For the modern Zeus brand, the Artemision Zeus offers a template for "authority without chaos." In a world saturated with loud, aggressive displays of power, this sculpture argues for the opposite: the most commanding presence is the one that does not need to shout. This visual strategy is directly translatable to contemporary branding--think of a CEO who speaks softly but commands absolute attention, or a nation that projects strength through calm diplomacy rather than aggression.
