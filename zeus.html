Client file

Zeus

Titlezeus
Client: Zeus, King of the Gods God of the Sky, Thunder and Cosmic Order

Campaign Strategy: Authority & Order

Campaign Goal: To show Zeus in a light Zeus is more than just a sensational story from
popular culture. Zeus is the symbol of being in control keeping people safe and bringing
order to the universe. This is what Zeus is about and it is something that people still need
today.

Zeus is in charge of what the gods and humans do. As the one who makes sure everyone
follows the rules and keeps society in order Zeus real strength comes from peoples need for
justice, protection and a fair system. This campaign looks at two Classical sculptures to see
how old artists showed these values in their work. It also shows how these same ideas can
be used to create an image of Zeus that is calm strong and fair. Zeus is about justice and
keeping people safe and that is what this campaign is all about Zeus and his role, in keeping
order.


Artwork 1: Zeus of Artemision

Date: c. 460 BCE (Early Classical Period)
Medium: Bronze
Dimensions: 2.09 m height

Location: National Archaeological Museum, Athens

Discovery: Found in an ancient shipwreck off Cape Artemision
Brand Strategy Analysis

The Zeus of Artemision is not a sculpture it is a picture that shows power in a simple way.
This work of art shows a change in Greek art: it changed from being stiff and boxy to being
more real and full of life. The way the god is standing with his weight moving from his back
leg to his leg is a new way of posing that would later become the standard for showing
power in a natural way in Western art. The Zeus of Artemision is an example of this.

Our brand analysis finds three visual things, in The Zeus of Artemision:
1. The Dynamic Pose: Zeus stands with his arms stretched out ready to throw a lightning
bolt. He looks balanced and in control unlike statues of gods that show them straining. This
shows that real power does not need to show effort. A simple move can control everything.

2. The Idealized PhysiqueHis face is intense. He looks like a person but also like a god.
This makes him someone we can relate to but also look up to. He is like us. Stronger.

3. The Moment of Action: The second before he throws the lightning bolt is full of excitement.
He is, in control of his power not controlled by it. This shows that he is a god who knows
what he is doing.

Campaign Relevance
For the modern Zeus brand, the Artemision Zeus offers a template for "authority without
chaos." In a world saturated with loud, aggressive displays of power, this sculpture argues
for the opposite: the most commanding presence is the one that does not need to shout.
This visual strategy is directly translatable to contemporary branding--think of a CEO who
speaks softly but commands absolute attention, or a nation that projects strength through
calm diplomacy rather than aggression.

Artwork 2: Head of Zeus from Mylasa

Date: c. 350­340 BCE (Late Classical Period)

Medium: Marble from Mt. Pentelikon near Athens

Dimensions: 48 x 26 cm (over-life-size)

Location: Museum of Fine Arts, Boston (Accession Number 04.12)

Provenance: Mylasa in Caria (southwest Turkey)
Brand Strategy Analysis

This huge marble head is like a look at the famous statue of Zeus made by Phidias a long
time ago. People thought that statue was one of the amazing things in the world. Even
though we can't see the original anymore this head helps us understand what it looked like
and shows us another side of the Zeus brand.

Our analysis of the brand finds three things that make it strong:

The Balanced Look: The hair, beard and face are perfectly symmetrical. This makes the
statue look calm and powerful. It's not the face of someone who rules with fear. Someone
who is wise and strong. The message is that stability and reliability are important for
leadership.
The Face: The face is turned a little to the right and looks calm not angry. This is a god who
doesn't need to be angry to get what he wants. He is so powerful that he doesn't need to
show it. This is the face of someone who brings justice, not revenge.

The Big Size: This head is 48 cm big which is bigger than life. The size makes you pay
attention and show respect. It says that this is someone important not just a regular king but
someone who makes sure everything is, in order.
Campaign Relevance

The Artemision Zeus is an example for the Zeus brand today. It shows that you can be in
charge without being loud. In a world where people often show off with aggressive displays
this sculpture proves that being calm and quiet can be just as powerful. A CEO who speaks
quietly but gets everyones attention is an example.. Think of a country that shows its
strength through calm talks rather than fights. The Zeus brand can use this idea to show that
it is strong and in charge without being loud.

The Artemision Zeus gives the Zeus brand an idea. It is about being strong without being
loud. This is useful today. Many things are loud and showy.. Being calm can be strong too. A
quiet CEO can get attention. A country can be strong with talks. The Zeus brand can use
this.

For the Zeus brand the Artemision Zeus is a good example. It shows authority without being
loud. This is a message. In todays world many people try to be loud to be heard.. Being quiet
and calm can be just as good. A CEO who is quiet but, in charge is an example. So is a
country that is strong but calm.
