Agency brief

This is not just a popularity contest.

Divine Rebrand Agency is where the Olympian gods prove that their powers still affect people today.

Each god gets a client file. In that file, the god uses artworks of themselves, their symbols, and their myths as evidence of what they can still do.

Aphrodite uses images of beauty and desire. Demeter uses images of grain, ritual, and seasonal return. The other gods will do the same: each page turns ancient art into a modern campaign for divine power.

How the agency works

Every god needs a case.

Visual proof

The images keep working

Agency method

Every claim needs evidence.

1

Hook

Start with a modern slogan, urgent notice, crisis, or pitch. The god needs a reason to speak now.

2

Evidence file

Name the artwork, date, medium, location if known, mythological subject, and why it fits the god's campaign.

3

Proof

Use pose, body, gesture, composition, symbols, scale, material, style, and viewer response.

4

Comparison

Compare another god, object, period, or medium so the campaign feels like an argument instead of a poster.

Agency records

Evidence standards for every client

Public claim

Every client needs a clear promise: love, authority, survival, performance, order, or control over nature.

References

Each client file keeps track of image sources, museum information, object dates, media, and scholarly references.

Consistency

Each client file follows the same structure: crisis, campaign slogan, main artwork, visual analysis, comparison, and final pitch.
