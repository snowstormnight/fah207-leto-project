Project thesis

This is not just a popularity contest.

Divine Rebrand Agency is the office where the Olympian gods hire campaign teams to prove that their powers are still alive. The crisis is simple: Olympus is losing attention, so each god must build a public case using art.

Every client file acts like a promotional pitch, but the "proof" is visual. A statue, vase, relief, temple image, or ritual object becomes evidence for why that god still matters.

The serious art-history work happens through the artworks. Each page uses objects from the course or closely related examples to explain medium, pose, iconography, attributes, style, ritual context, viewer response, and mythological meaning.

In other words, each subpage asks: if a god had to prove their power today, which ancient image would they use as evidence?

How to read the site

Every page has two voices.

Campaign voice

The creative layer

Slogans, urgent dispatches, crisis files, and dramatic titles make each god feel like a client in a modern agency. This is the campaign layer that makes the website more original than a normal essay.

Art-history voice

The evidence layer

Formal analysis explains how the artworks actually create divine meaning: pose, iconography, material, period style, viewer experience, and comparison with other gods or media.

Final purpose

The argument

The project shows that the gods survive because their images keep being copied, reinterpreted, and used to make viewers feel awe, attraction, fear, discipline, or devotion.

Client files

Six gods, six image strategies

Each god has a different visual strategy. The campaign is playful, but the page should mostly explain artworks using art-historical terms.

Ready page Aphrodite

Make desire visible.

Aphrodite's campaign turns love and beauty into an argument about looking: nudity, contrapposto, modest gesture, marble surface, and viewer response.

Open Aphrodite Case
Authority Zeus

Power needs a body.

Use thunderbolt iconography, scale, throne imagery, heroic anatomy, and public command to show how Zeus becomes visual authority.

Leadership Hera / Poseidon

Choose the crown or the sea.

Hera can focus on queenship, marriage, and status; Poseidon can focus on motion, violence, horses, sea power, and bronze action.

Performance Dionysus

Lose control, gain followers.

Track wine, theatre, masks, maenads, satyrs, and vase imagery to show how Dionysus sells divine ecstasy and social reversal.

Survival Demeter

No harvest, no civilization.

Her campaign connects agriculture, motherhood, mourning, fertility, and ritual to images of grain, torches, Persephone/Kore, and seasonal return.

How every subpage should work

40 percent campaign, 60 percent art history.

1

Hook

Start with a modern slogan, ad, crisis, or pitch. This makes the page creative and memorable.

2

Object file

Name the artwork, date, medium, location if known, mythological subject, and why it fits the god's campaign.

3

Visual proof

Analyze pose, body, gesture, composition, iconography, scale, material, style, and viewer experience.

4

Comparison

Compare another god, object, period, or medium so the page feels like an argument instead of a poster.
