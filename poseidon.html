Theoretical Foundations of Regional Mythological Shifts

The religious concept about the supreme deity changed when it moved from Proto-Indo-European starts into specific localized Greek places (Duev, 2019). This big shift gives a good basic theory about how standard myths change because of regional, historical, and geopolitical impacts over time. Specifically, the growing of the cult of an Indo-European Sky Father is very different from the assumed Proto-Indo-European tradition, and this religious cult grew under different impacts that made big changes in his cult in the Aegean area at the time of the aoidoi (Duev, 2019, pp. 121, 141). During this historical time of cultural change, the syncretism that started in the Mycenaean time made a different result on the west coast of Asia Minor and the islands that go toward it and mainland Greece (Duev, 2019, p. 141). Inside this growing divine setup, the daughters of Zeus are the goddesses Athena and Aphrodite, and the son of Zeus is Apollo (Duev, 2019, p. 121). This big system change shows that old divine setups were not static, but instead they reacted to local social-political areas and this relates to how other high gods like Poseidon were also viewed differently in separate towns.

Historical Application and the Poseidon Context
A direct historical use of this local change phenomenon is seen in how the specific myth fight between Athena and Poseidon was structurally changed by the Athenian people across different centuries to match and show their changing sea power and money focus. The changing of this famous Athenian myth can be joined with the history of the city itself, so we are allowed to think that the changes in the myth really show the changes experienced by Athenian sea power (Patay-HorvÃ¡th, 2015, p. 360). This deep study suggests strong connections between Athenian history and the changing of the myth, which is an updated, revised, and short version of a 2002 study that has bibliography references up to 2007 (Patay-HorvÃ¡th, 2015, p. 353). In this famous fight, the symbolic style of the gifts is made very clear in the case of the water basin, which was mostly called thalassa, and it must have shown the navigation, sea trade, maritime power of Poseidon, or some close concept (Patay-HorvÃ¡th, 2015, p. 356). Therefore, the myth fight involving Poseidon for the city was not just a static story, but it was changed by the community to work as a story tool that showed their political goals.

Comparative Analysis of Localized Cult Development

Comparative Analysis of Localized Cult Development
When studying these changes, it is clear that religious setups react directly to the needs of the people that pray to them. The change of the Sky Father cult shows that outside geopolitical push can rewrite basic beliefs, and this matches the way that the Athenian state changed its main city myth about Poseidon to align with current sea power (Duev, 2019; Patay-HorvÃ¡th, 2015). The aoidoi worked as workers of this change by putting new regional facts into poetry, which allowed the changing identities of mainland Greece and Asia Minor to take place in the wider religious view (Duev, 2019, p. 141). Also, the Athenian translation of the thalassa of Poseidon as a sign of sea power shows that physical facts, like sea trade and money growth, needed religious support through divine stories (Patay-HorvÃ¡th, 2015, p. 356). By looking at the specific differences between early Indo-European traditions and later old versions, researchers can see a clear way of real change where myth serves history and the power of Poseidon gets remade to fit the navy.

Sociopolitical Implications of Mythological Remodeling

Sociopolitical Implications of Mythological Remodeling
The changing of these myths had big results for the social balance and political messages of old communities. For the Greek people, making specific family lines, like saying Athena and Aphrodite are the daughters of Zeus and Apollo is the son of Zeus, helped put the gods into a clear city setup that local governments could use (Duev, 2019, p. 121). This divine setup copied the real political changing that happened during the move from the Mycenaean time to the classical time. In Athens, the smooth changing of the fight between Athena and Poseidon allowed the state to keep a feel of history continuation even when its money focus changed heavily between farming traditions and sea control (Patay-HorvÃ¡th, 2015, p. 360). Because the changes in the myth really showed the changes experienced by Athenian sea power, the story worked as a regular talk on city identity and the respect given to Poseidon changed when the city needed to boast about its ships. Lastly, both the system changing of the high Sky Father and the targeted updates to the Athenian fight story of Poseidon show that old myths were moving cultural papers made by geopolitical need.
